EPAAS Conference 2017, Mumbai

Travel and Hotels

A quick primer for those traveling to Mumbai.

Mumbai: Mumbai is one of the biggest cities in the world with a population of more than 15 million. There are lots of places to visit from ancient Buddhist caves to British monuments and plenty of Cafes and Bars.

Hotels: Hotels in Mumbai are generally expensive, so avoid staying very close to the venue. There are a lot of hotels around the airport and that might be the best option for getting a deal. The venue would be around 30-45 mins for the airport based on where you are. Cleartrip might have the best deals. AirBNB might also have good options.

Getting Around: Uber and Ola Cabs are app based taxi services that will work very well. We 100% recommend these over local taxis and auto-rickshaws (tuk-tuks) which are likely to over-charge out station passengers

Venue: The venue is at the Mysore Association Auditorium and has all modern facilities you would expect from a venue. It is centrally located and easily accessible from all corners.
